ABOUT Gabriel Medina

BRAZILIAN PROFESSIONAL SURFER

December 22, 1993

Born in São Sebastião, São Paulo, Gabriel has begun surfing at the age of 9. At the age of 11, he won his first national championship, Rip Curl Grom Search in Rio de Janeiro. Few years later, at the age of 15, Gabriel became the youngest athlete to win the World Junior Tour (ASP). Currently, he is the youngest Brazilian to join the ASP World Champions Tour (WCT). Medina not only conquered many Brazilian championships, such as Volcom Sub-14, Quicksilver King of Groms and Rip Curl Grom Search, but also won the state championship three times. In California, he was the second at the Volcom Internacional Sub-14, while in Ecuador, vice-champion of the Amateur World Sub-16 Championship. In July 2009, Medina signed his first contract with Rip Curl. It was beginning of his professional surfing career - only 10 days later, he won World Championship. From then, Gabriel shares waves with the world top surfers. By winning the Quiksilver Pro Gold Coast, Medina became the highest ranked surfer on the list. His sources credit him as being the second person to have executed a maneuver called the "Backflip", which to date has never been performed in competition.
